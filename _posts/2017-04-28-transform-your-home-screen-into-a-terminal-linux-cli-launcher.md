<p>
  The Google Play Store features many launchers, of all sorts, ready to make your life easier.&nbsp; Whether it's Apex Launcher, Evie Launcher, or Nova
  Launcher, there are a lot of third-party launchers for you to choose from.&nbsp; At the base-level though, these launchers start out &#8212; thematically
  &#8212; quite similar.&nbsp; However, you can customize them with beautiful icon packs, themes, widgets, and more.&nbsp; As you may know, Android's built on
  the Linux kernel.&nbsp; Thus, it has a hidden shell functionality &#8212; which, fans of GNU/Linux may be pleased to give a try.&nbsp; A full Linux terminal
  environment on an Android phone?&nbsp; That, definitely, sounds interesting.&nbsp; But, what if you could replace your stock launcher with a launcher that
  mimics the Linux terminal interface?&nbsp; Luckily, you can &#8212; thanks to the Linux CLI Launcher.
</p>
<p>
  Linux CLI Launcher for Android replaces your home screen with a minimalist terminal-style interface, which uses commands to control basically
  everything.&nbsp; You can call, manage your contacts, and/or text using simple commands.&nbsp; Aside from supporting some basic GNU/Linux commands, the
  application offers the ability to create aliases&hellip;&nbsp; Running your favourite games and/or applications effortlessly.
</p>
<!-- excerptBreak -->
<p>
  You don't have to be a GNU/Linux nerd nor know all of the commands.&nbsp; The launcher provides a built-in command suggestion feature.&nbsp; Navigating the
  launcher's relatively easy.&nbsp; With some practice, it helps you to understand some of the more basic terminal commands &#8212; should you try to run a
  distribution on your desktop and/or laptop later on.
</p>
