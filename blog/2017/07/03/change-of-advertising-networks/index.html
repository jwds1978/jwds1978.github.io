Change of Advertising Networks

Count Words — Reading Time
by James Stewart
 Published:  03 July 2017 @ 14:16:32 EDT
Updated:  04 July 2017 @ 05:21:01 EDT  Corrected Payza to ePayments
Location:  The Donovan, Sudbury, Ontario, Canada

 

As you may or may not have noticed, I've made some changes to the on-site ad units.  While I've had no policy violations with my AdSense account, I've decided to disable them for awhile — in favour of Anonymous Ads and Propeller Ads ad units.

A-ADS is a bitcoin online advertising network that doesn't collect personal data.  Propeller Ads is a full and self-service advertising network, providing comprehensive and industry leading ad-serving and optimization technologies, for online marketers and Web publishers.

 

I've been testing both networks for a little bit now.  Thus far, I'd have to say that I'm happy with my decision.  With A-ADS, I'm using it as a drop-in replacement for the previous AdSense units.  With Propeller Ads, I'm making use of their pop-under and mobile push-up formats.

Neither the pop-under nor mobile push-up units should be displayed more than once per any given session, minimizing any possible annoyance or intrusion, as related to UX.  The banner units are unchanged — no less/more than prior — having simply replaced the AdSense units.

 

My motivation was, quite simply, a financial one.  It costs more to run this Web site than most people seem to realize.  Although I was getting some revenue via AdSense, it was rather insulting — to say the least.  Even with several thousand views each day, I was only making something in the order of <$1.00 per month…  Whiskey — Tango — Foxtrot?!?

Given that, I was losing significant amounts of money, paying for everything out-of-pocket.  At this point, since making the move to A-ADS and Propeller Ads, I'm now making enough to pay the majority of my operating costs via ad-generated revenue.  As time goes on, I'd hope to be actually turning a profit.  I don't expect to become a billionaire or anything, but a healthy revenue-stream — being, otherwise, unemployable and all — would be nice though.

 

What I also like about both networks, is that I can ultimately receive payment via bitcoin.  A-ADS pays, directly to my wallet of choice, on a daily basis — 0.001 XBT minimum.  Propeller Ads pays, to may ePayments account, on a monthly basis — $100.00 USD minimum.  From ePayments, I'm able to withdraw to my bitcoin wallet of choice.

Both networks offer other payment methods, with varying thresholds, as well.  However, personally, I much prefer to be paid via bitcoin.  From my bitcoin wallet(s), I'm able to load my Cryptopay pre-paid credit card(s).  And, in-turn, I'm then able to pay the bills. 

 

Regardless of whether you're looking for an AdSense alternative, whether by choice or force (i.e. AdSense suspension), I'd encourage you to consider A-ADS and/or Propeller Ads.
